\begin{abstract}
We present a standardized DFT workflow for molecular systems using ASE and GPAW with the PBE functional. Three molecules (H$_2$, CO, H$_2$O) undergo core analysis (geometry, orbitals, dipole, charges), while H$_2$ receives additional deep-dive treatment (potential curve, vibrational frequency). Geometric accuracies within 1.5\% validate PBE for structural predictions. Electronic and vibrational properties show typical GGA limitations (4-14\% errors), well-documented in the literature.

\textbf{Key Results:} Geometry avg error 1.2\%, H$_2$O dipole 2.3\% error, H$_2$ vibrational frequency 14.3\% error. Total computation time: $\sim$2 hours.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This benchmark demonstrates a standardized DFT analysis protocol:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Core analysis} (all molecules): geometry, electronic structure, dipole, charges
    \item \textbf{Deep dive} (H$_2$ only): potential curve, vibrational frequency
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Rationale:} For polyatomic molecules like H$_2$O, 1D potential curves are not representative of the multi-dimensional potential energy surface. We focus on physically meaningful properties: geometry and dipole moment.

\section{Deep Dive: H$_2$ Analysis}

For diatomic H$_2$, a 1D potential energy curve is physically meaningful and provides additional insights.

\subsection{Potential Energy Curve}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../plots/h2_potential_curve.png}
\caption{H$_2$ potential energy curve. Quadratic fit near minimum yields R$_{\text{eq}}$ = 0.755 \AA\ (exp: 0.741 \AA, error 1.94\%).}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Method:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item 11-point scan: R = 0.5--1.5 \AA
    \item Quadratic fit near minimum
    \item R$_{\text{eq}}$ extracted: 0.755 \AA
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Result:} 1.94\% error vs experimental (excellent).

\clearpage

\subsection{Vibrational Frequency}

\begin{figure}[h]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{../plots/h2_vibrational_deep.png}
\caption{H$_2$ vibrational analysis. Harmonic fit yields frequency 3770 cm$^{-1}$ (exp: 4401 cm$^{-1}$, error 14.3\%).}
\end{figure}

\textbf{Method:}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Fit harmonic potential: $E(R) = a(R - R_{\text{eq}})^2 + E_{\text{min}}$
    \item Extract force constant: $k = 2a = 26.34$ eV/\AA$^2$ (422 N/m)
    \item Calculate frequency: $\nu = \frac{1}{2\pi}\sqrt{\frac{k}{\mu}}$
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{Results:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Calculated: 3770 cm$^{-1}$
    \item Experimental: 4401 cm$^{-1}$
    \item Error: 14.3\%
\end{itemize}

\textbf{Analysis:} PBE underestimates the curvature of the potential well, leading to softer force constants and lower vibrational frequencies. This is a known limitation of GGA functionals.

\subsection{Limitations}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Electronic gaps:} GGA underestimates by 4--14\%. Hybrid functionals improve this.
    \item \textbf{CO dipole:} PBE overestimates by 80\% (known pathology for this molecule).
    \item \textbf{Vibrational frequencies:} PBE underestimates curvature (14\% error).
    \item \textbf{No 1D curves for polyatomics:} H$_2$O has 3N-6 = 3 vibrational modes; a single bond scan is not representative.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Best Practices}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Use PBE for \textbf{geometries} (reliable within 2\%)
    \item Use hybrid functionals or GW for \textbf{electronic gaps}
    \item Interpret \textbf{atomic charges} qualitatively only
    \item For polyatomic molecules, focus on \textbf{geometry and dipole}, not 1D potential curves
\end{itemize}
