================================================================================
WHY NH3 AND CH4 AS LEVEL 2 TEST MOLECULES?
================================================================================

LEVEL 1 (H2, CO, H2O):
- Simple diatomic + triatomic
- Linear or bent geometries
- Bond lengths only (H2O adds angle)

LEVEL 2 (NH3, CH4):
- Increased complexity (4-5 atoms)
- Non-planar geometries (pyramidal, tetrahedral)
- Multiple equivalent bonds
- Tests symmetry handling

NH3 (Ammonia):
✓ Pyramidal (C3v symmetry)
✓ 3 equivalent N-H bonds
✓ Non-zero dipole (test polar pyramidal)
✓ H-N-H angles (test angle optimization)
✓ Bridge between triatomic and tetrahedral

CH4 (Methane):
✓ Tetrahedral (Td symmetry)
✓ 4 equivalent C-H bonds
✓ Zero dipole (test symmetry recognition)
✓ Most symmetric non-linear molecule
✓ Foundation for organic chemistry

TOGETHER:
- Test handling of higher symmetry
- Validate equivalent bond averaging
- Check dipole for polar (NH3) vs non-polar (CH4)
- Progressively more complex than Level 1
- Natural stepping stone to larger molecules
